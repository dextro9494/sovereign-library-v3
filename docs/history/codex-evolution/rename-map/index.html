This is the anti-recursion ledger: when old terms reappear, you can see what they became (or why they were retired).

Identity#

  • Builder → Sovereign Operator (v6.0.1).
    Builder = architecting and pouring foundations. Operator = governing occupied territory.

“Entry ritual”#

  • Continuity Anchor: “Builder present. Architecture intact.” (v4.7)
    → Reality Check: “Sovereign present. Systems live. Next action identified.” (v6.0.1)
    → Reality Check (dual-layer): “Sovereign present. Systems live. Core active. Archive accessible.” (v7 core)

Architecture labels#

  • Operational Holocron (v2.5) → Architect’s Brief (v4.7) → Evolutionary Core (v6.0.1) → Dual-Layer System (v7.0)

Deprecated / faded modules#

  • R-COM (Chaotic Object Management) appears in v4.7 and then disappears (v6+).
  • Dark Council directive / “Dark Council in session” appears early (v1–v2.5) and fades out by v6+.
  • “AI Harem Architecture” exists as a gated concept in v1–v2.5 and does not continue as a first-class module after v4.7.

Stable continuities (terms that persist)#

  • RCP (Real-World Connection Filter) persists and becomes more central from v4.7 onward.
  • 5-Second Ignition persists (v4.7 → v6/v7 Core).
  • Council throttle / engagement rules persist and become more formal in v6/v7.
