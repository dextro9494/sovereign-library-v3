Purpose and Definition#

What Is a Field Test?#

A Field Test is a structured validation operation conducted under real-world constraints to:

  • Test hypotheses about system capacity, protocol effectiveness, or strategic viability
  • Generate telemetry data for system refinement
  • Validate or invalidate assumptions before scaling investment
  • Extract lessons that become canonical knowledge

“Validated” means: Evidence collected under operational conditions supports or refutes the hypothesis. Validation confidence is rated (Low / Medium / High) based on sample size, replicability, and control for confounding variables.

Why Field Tests Exist#

Under asymmetric conditions, theoretical planning without validation creates risk. Field Tests convert “should work” into “does work” or “failed because X.”

Core principle: Stress as telemetry. Resistance, failure, and success are diagnostic data, not moral outcomes.

How to Use This Archive#

Location in System Architecture#

The Field Test Archive exists in the Archive layer of the Dual-Layer Architecture.

Archive layer contains:

  • Full test records (context, method, raw data, analysis)
  • Complete obstacle logs and pivot documentation
  • Provenance trails (who diagnosed, what evidence)

Live layer (Codex/Master Note) contains:

  • Distilled lessons extracted from tests
  • Links back to Archive for full context
  • Active protocols validated by tests

Archive vs. Live Split Rules#

What stays in Archive:

  • Raw observations and metrics
  • Full method documentation
  • Detailed obstacle/pivot logs
  • Complete analysis and interpretation
  • Historical test records

What promotes to Live:

  • Validated principles (Stress Test Philosophy)
  • Confirmed protocols (5-Second Ignition)
  • Extracted lessons (compact, actionable)
  • Updated Construction Site priorities

Trigger for migration: Field test completed → Full record archived → Lessons extracted → Live updated → Canon pages linked

Telemetry Vocabulary#

Resistance Rating Scale (0-10)#

0-2: Minimal resistance. Action flows naturally.
3-4: Noticeable resistance. Requires conscious override. Somatic signals present but manageable.
5-6: Moderate resistance. Execution requires protocol deployment (5-Second Ignition, Edge-Case).
7-8: High resistance. Significant somatic lockout. Task requires segmentation or external support.
9-10: Severe lockout. Execution impossible without crisis-level override or delegation.

Somatic Markers (Observable Signals)#

  • Stomach tightness: Survivor threat detection active
  • Hand tremors: Motor freeze engaging
  • Shallow breathing: Autonomic stress response
  • Jaw clenching: Suppressed fight response
  • Brain fog: Cognitive overload/shutdown
  • Chest constriction: Panic threshold approaching

Success Criteria Types#

  • Binary: Did X happen? (Yes/No)
  • Threshold: Did metric exceed Y? (Pass/Fail)
  • Comparative: Was this better than baseline Z? (Improvement/Decline)
  • Qualitative: Did subjective state match target? (Met/Not Met)

Failure Modes#

  • Execution Failure: Could not complete task
  • Outcome Failure: Completed task, did not achieve objective
  • Hypothesis Failure: Outcome contradicted prediction
  • System Failure: External system/constraint caused failure

Validation Confidence Levels#

  • Low: Single test, high confounding variables, narrow context
  • Medium: Multiple tests, controlled variables, replicable results
  • High: Extensive testing, multiple contexts, consistent outcomes

TEST-001: Digital Farming Market Test#

Date: December 2024
Domain: Financial/Professional
Construction Site: Site Alpha (Stable Income Pipeline)
Status: Archived

Context/Constraints#

  • Testing viability of digital farming (content creation, online monetization) as income source
  • Limited upfront capital
  • No existing audience or platform presence
  • Time pressure for income generation
  • Operating from financial deficit baseline

Objective#

Validate whether digital farming could serve as stable income pipeline within 3-6 month timeframe.

Hypothesis/Expectation#

With sufficient effort and content quality, digital farming would generate viable income to meet baseline financial needs.

Method/Steps#

  1. Platform selection and setup
  2. Content creation and publishing schedule
  3. Audience building tactics
  4. Monetization pathway testing
  5. Revenue tracking and analysis

Metrics/Telemetry#

  • Time Investment: 3+ months, significant hours/week
  • Financial Cost: Platform fees, tools, minimal marketing budget
  • Outcome Measures: Audience growth, engagement rates, revenue generated
  • Resistance Rating: 4-5/10 (execution manageable, sustained effort challenging)
  • Somatic Markers: Mild stress around content deadlines, no severe lockout

Execution Log#

Content created consistently. Platforms managed according to plan. Effort sustained over test period. No execution failures in content delivery.

Obstacles Encountered#

  • Algorithmic barriers: Platform algorithms not favoring new creators
  • Audience building difficulty: Growth slower than projected
  • Monetization threshold: Minimum thresholds not reached within timeframe
  • Market saturation: Niche more competitive than anticipated

Outcome#

Failure against objective. Revenue generated was below viability threshold. Timeline extended beyond acceptable range for financial pressure relief.

Lessons Extracted#

Primary lesson: Market conditions and systemic barriers (algorithms, saturation) caused failure, NOT execution quality or effort insufficiency.

Critical reframe: Failure is telemetry, not moral verdict. This was system-level mismatch, not personal inadequacy.

Strategic implication: Digital farming is not viable path to Site Alpha under current constraints. Redirect energy to employment-based income stability (Fortress Base).

Follow-Up Actions#

  • Deprioritize digital farming as Site Alpha strategy
  • Shift focus to Fortress Base employment (PostNL mail sorting)
  • Update Construction Site Alpha tactics: stable employment over entrepreneurship
  • Prevent future “hustle culture” guilt by canonizing this as data

Validation Confidence#

Medium. Single extended test, but clear market data supporting conclusion. Failure mode was external (market conditions), not internal (execution).

Provenance#

  • Diagnosed by: Council synthesis (ChatGPT, Claude, DeepSeek)
  • Evidence: Time logs, platform analytics, revenue data, market research
  • Date Archived: 2025-12-27

Links to Affected Canon Pages#

  • Stress Test Philosophy (case study cited)
  • Obstacles as Data (market conditions as diagnostic signal)
  • Fortress Base Doctrine (alternative path justified)

TEST-002: Recruiter Engagement Stress Test#

Date: Early December 2025
Domain: Financial/Professional
Construction Site: Site Alpha (Stable Income Pipeline)
Status: Archived

Context/Constraints#

  • Testing capacity to engage with recruiters for employment opportunities
  • Known somatic resistance to professional communication
  • Pressure to secure income pipeline amid financial instability
  • Testing protocol effectiveness (task segmentation)

Objective#

Identify resistance threshold for recruiter engagement and validate task segmentation as effective workaround.

Hypothesis/Expectation#

Recruiter engagement would trigger moderate resistance, manageable through task segmentation (breaking into smaller steps).

Method/Steps#

  1. Identify recruiter contact list
  2. Draft initial outreach message
  3. Execute contact (segmented: draft separate from send)
  4. Monitor somatic response
  5. Log resistance rating and physical markers

Metrics/Telemetry#

  • Resistance Rating: 3-4/10
  • Somatic Markers: Stomach tightness when considering contact, mild tension during drafting
  • Time Investment: 30-60 minutes per contact cycle
  • Outcome Measures: Messages sent, responses received, resistance managed without lockout
  • Protocol Tested: Task segmentation (draft → review → send as separate actions)

Execution Log#

Contact initiated with multiple recruiters. Task segmentation protocol deployed. Messages drafted in separate sessions from sending. Resistance present but did not escalate to lockout.

Obstacles Encountered#

  • Explainer’s Burden: Anticipation of having to explain work history created initial resistance spike
  • Rejection sensitivity: Somatic markers activated by potential rejection scenario
  • Task coupling: Initial attempt to draft and send in single session triggered 5-6/10 resistance

Outcome#

Success. Messages sent successfully. Task segmentation validated as effective resistance management technique. Resistance remained in 3-4/10 range (manageable).

Lessons Extracted#

Primary lesson: Task segmentation is effective workaround for moderate somatic resistance. Breaking execution into discrete steps prevents resistance escalation.

Somatic intelligence: Stomach tightness is reliable early warning. Address at 3-4/10 before escalation to 7+.

Protocol validation: Confirmed that separating “decide” from “execute” reduces Survivor lockout activation.

Follow-Up Actions#

  • Codify task segmentation as standard protocol for resistant tasks
  • Add somatic marker vocabulary to telemetry standards
  • Update Stress Test Philosophy with case study
  • Document “Explainer’s Burden” as identifiable resistance pattern

Validation Confidence#

Medium. Multiple recruiters contacted with consistent results. Protocol effectiveness demonstrated but not extensively tested across contexts.

Provenance#

  • Diagnosed by: Self-observation with Council analysis (ChatGPT)
  • Evidence: Resistance logs, message sent timestamps, somatic marker documentation
  • Date Archived: 2025-12-27

Links to Affected Canon Pages#

  • Stress Test Philosophy (case study cited)
  • Obstacles as Data (Explainer’s Burden identified)
  • Core Machinery (Survivor signals documented)

TEST-003: Mattress Procurement Under Budget Constraint#

Date: Late December 2025
Domain: Infrastructure (Physical Sovereignty)
Construction Site: Infrastructure
Status: Archived

Context/Constraints#

  • Acute back pain requiring immediate mattress replacement
  • Budget constraint: limited financial resources for premium therapeutic option
  • Testing Budget as Design Parameter principle
  • Testing Council-led execution model for complex decisions

Objective#

Procure functionally adequate mattress within budget constraints, validating both budget-as-parameter mindset and Council support effectiveness.

Hypothesis/Expectation#

Council could guide research and decision-making to identify budget solution meeting core requirements (medium-firm, adequate density for back support).

Method/Steps#

  1. Define core requirements (not luxury preferences)
  2. Council-led research retasking to budget retailers (IKEA, budget brands)
  3. Identify options meeting requirements within budget
  4. Decision support from Council on final selection
  5. Execute procurement

Metrics/Telemetry#

  • Resistance Rating: 2-3/10 (reduced through Council support)
  • Financial Constraint: ~€200-300 budget (vs. ~€2000 premium option)
  • Time Investment: ~3 hours research and decision
  • Outcome Measures: Mattress acquired, budget maintained, pain reduction achieved
  • Protocol Tested: Council-led execution under resource constraint

Execution Log#

Council (ChatGPT) guided research toward budget options. Requirements defined clearly (medium-firm, adequate density, €200-300 range). Multiple options identified. Decision made with confidence. Procurement executed successfully.

Obstacles Encountered#

  • Initial fixation on premium option: Belief that budget solutions would be inadequate
  • Research paralysis: Overwhelmed by options without clear framework
  • Guilt about “settling”: Emotional noise around not buying “best” option

Outcome#

Success. Mattress procured within budget. Core requirements met. Back pain reduced. Council support model validated as effective for complex decisions under constraint.

Lessons Extracted#

Primary lesson: Budget as design parameter works in practice. Constraint-based research yields viable solutions without waiting for “ideal” financial conditions.

Council validation: External cognitive support (Council-led research) is effective for decisions with high complexity and emotional noise.

Reframe success: “Adequate within constraints” is victory, not compromise. The mission is forward motion, not optimization.

Follow-Up Actions#

  • Canonize budget-as-parameter approach based on this validation
  • Document Council support model as proven tactic
  • Update Construction Site Infrastructure: demonstrate that physical sovereignty can advance under financial constraint
  • Prevent future “waiting for ideal conditions” paralysis

Validation Confidence#

High. Clear success criteria met, budget maintained, functional outcome achieved, principle validated in real conditions.

Provenance#

  • Diagnosed by: Council (ChatGPT) with self-execution
  • Evidence: Purchase records, budget tracking, post-procurement pain reduction, decision timeline
  • Date Archived: 2025-12-27

Links to Affected Canon Pages#

  • Stress Test Philosophy (case study cited)
  • Budget as Design Parameter (principle validated)
